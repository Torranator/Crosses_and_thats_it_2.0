    The history of Converse began in 1908, when Marcus Mills Converse founded a shoe factory in Malden, Massachusetts. Initially, the company specialized in the production of work shoes, especially for miners and builders. In 1917, the company was renamed the Converse Rubber Shoe Company, as the main focus was on the production of rubber shoes. In 1921, Converse introduced its first basketball shoes, which quickly became popular among players. This led to the fact that in 1936 the company decided to focus on the production of sports shoes. In the following years, the brand became associated with basketball and became one of the most recognizable in the world of sports. In 1986, Converse teamed up with Nike to expand its market and improve its products. Since then, the Converse brand has become even more famous thanks to collaboration with various celebrities, athletes and fashion designers. Today Converse continues to produce a variety of sports shoes and clothing, and is also one of the key players in the youth fashion market.
